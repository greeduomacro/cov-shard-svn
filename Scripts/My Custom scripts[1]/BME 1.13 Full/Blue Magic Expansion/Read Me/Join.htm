    In order to become a Blue Mage you'll need to speak with Ben.

    he looks like this

    If you have less than 50.0 Forensics he'll off a New Haven style go train it quest. However, the reward for training Forensics is 51.0 rather than 50.0 like most other training quests. This allows you to start at 50.0 and still obtain the reward. Which is the Ring Of The Savant (some one the eval trainer gives) which is a Blessed ring with FC/FCR 1 & +3 int.

    You'll also need Magic Resistance. Forensics & Magic Resist work together like Magery & Eval Int or Necromancy & Spirit Speak. Also, there are Special Moves that can be learned. Much like Double Strike or even Bushido's Lightning Strike. In order you use those you'll have to be unarmed, Tactics & Wrestling would be required for those since otherwise without them you couldn't hit. More on this later.

    If you have more than 50.0 Forensics Ben will send a menu saying this
    Hello, my name is Ben and I am one of the Masters of Blue Magic. In order to become a blue mage, you must have at least 50 in forensics and you are required to train magic resist if you wish to be worth your weight. There are over twenty spells for you to learn as a blue mage, each one is unique and their effects widely very unlike any spell you have seen before.They can be learned throughout Ilshenar by interacting with various "special" monsters. Some spells are learned by being subject to their effects, while others can only be learned by studying the remains of the very special creatures (by using the "[bm study" and targeting the corpse). Lastly, for you types that like to get your hands dirty, there are a few moves that can be learned as well.

    If this appeals to you, I'd like to ask you a series of questions in order understand the nature of your soul. Thereby I will be able to tell you which mark is required for you to become a blue mage. Mindful this is a life style and not just a quick pick me up. Becoming a blue mage will severely inhibit your ability to be a master mage in any other spellcasting ability.

    When you click ok you'll be asked a series of questions, depending on your answer you'll be sent to find one of four objects.
    Sky: Located in Req Volom, minor run but you'll have to deal with spiders and evil mages.

    Flame: Located in the Volcano, short run but dragonic spawn and you'll need Teleport.

    Gale: Located in one in the Kirin Passage, western side with the largest cavern, kind of dangerous.

    Spring: Located on the bridge between Compassion and Spirituality. Easiest to obtain.

    Stone: Located in the Rock Dungeon, longest run but tends to be the safest.

    Only your Blue Mage can see the item and simply running up to it will cause it to pop into your pack. Once you have it you need to mark it as a quest item (like any mondain's obtain quest) click on your self to display your context menu and click on Toggle Quest Item, then target the scroll. Then talk to Ben. You'll be given one last chance to confirm this is what you want.

    Skill Lock
    Blue Mage operates almost if it were a class and has strict requirements. At no time are you allowed to have more than 50.0 as your base skill value in Magery, Necromancy, Chiveraly, Spellweaving, Mysticism, or Taming related skills. You are allowed (and encouraged) to use items to increase your relative skill, such as a Tome of Lost Knowledge. Should your base skill be higher than 50.0 upon joining or casting a spell it will be lowered.

    Ultimately this greatly effects which items you'll wear. Magery increasing rings and bracelets for instance are a must if you plan to use Mark & Recall. Draconic or legendary pets cannot be used but though Bushido you can obtain and use a Lesser Hiryu, you'll just have a limitation on your Vet skills preventing you from properly healing it.

    By intent, you cannot have it all and with the abundance of pokemon style shards, you're debuffs combined with a super pet are simply too powerful for a single player.
